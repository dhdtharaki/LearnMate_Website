LEARNMATE

Learning Companion for Autistic Children

LearnMate is a web application designed to support autistic children by enhancing their cognitive, metacognitive, affective, and psychomotor learning domains. The system analyzes each child's unique abilities, detects their developmental levels, and provides personalized recommendations to help them grow and learn more effectively.
Beyond supporting the children, LearnMate also aims to raise awareness among parents, teachers, and peers—offering insights on how to better engage with autistic children and support their development in meaningful ways.

Research Domain

Understanding the landscape of our research regarding ASD.

Understanding and supporting children with atypical learning needs has become a promising area of research. Apart from that, several studies are inclined to investigate how certain technologies, along with machine learning, may help in raising engagement, emotional development, and cognitive, meta-cognitive, and psychomotor skills in such children. This literature review looks at some recent efforts working in each of these learning domains and how adaptive systems develop customized experiences to support children with ASD.

Cognitive Domain:

Several authors stressed the use of technology to assist cognitive development in children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). The Kiddie Grid presents an intervention where approaches are individualized, and tools like gamification, virtual reality, and AI are used to engage children better while also promoting cognitive skills in areas such as memory, problem-solving, and attention. The intervention also provides for feedback and involvement with the parents [1]. In the work of Francys Oyuela and Oscar Paz, AI-powered robot interventions were proposed, with an initial focus on children with Down syndrome, but also relevant to ASD children. These robots used deep learning, reinforcement learning, and speech recognition to dynamically adapt to the child's responses, helping to maximize engagement and communication. The article by Kaushik Vakadkar et al. addresses machine learning models such as SVM, Decision Trees, KNN, and deep learning for the early diagnosis of ASD from behavioral and genetic data, with another layer of support for early cognition. Mostek J. looked at how AI and ML could further think and language development in children with ASD through the merging of psychological theories and adaptive systems. Jannelle Murray investigates instructional strategies such as individualized teaching, visual supports, assistive technology, and collaborative partnerships with parents to create a broadly structured and supportive learning environment [2].

Meta Cognitive Domain:

Ruan et al. came up with the Meta-Brain Hood, an intelligent tutoring system for analyzing facial emotional expressions using a facial emotion recognition algorithm to recognize both the basic and epistemic emotions. The study showed that a few emotional expressions are linked with metacognitive monitoring performance, suggesting that systems can aid self-awareness and reflection in children with ASD. According to Mitsea et al. (2022), different digital technologies such as robotics, mobile apps, serious games, or VR help people with ASD with metacognitive difficulties by promoting self-regulation, control of attention, and decision-making. Moraiti et al. (2023) carried out an exhaustive literature review on technology-enabled inclusion in schools, studying teacher perceptions and indicating web and mobile apps as having been effectively used to support memory, attention, and communication of ASD students. Moreover, Mitsea et al. (2020) referred to a multi-modal approach involving mindfulness, human-robot interaction, cooperative games, and programming activities that foster the growth of metacognitive abilities like self-reflection and emotional regulation [3] [4].

Affective Domain:

The affective domain, basically the emotions, attitudes, social skills, and motivation necessary for learning, in children with ASD-who tend to have deficits in recognizing and regulating these emotions of utmost importance. The latest developments in affective computing and artificial intelligence have shown tremendous potential. For instance, CNNs and the MMD algorithm have been proposed to enhance emotion recognition based on facial expressions [5]. ANNs have been used with more success than classical methods for the prediction of learners' outcomes and the adaptation of their learning pathway [6]. In addition, avatar-based serious gaming approaches have enhanced social communication by replicating gestures and expressions. The pressure-sensing keypad, an assistive technology, helps children with sensory sensitivity and motor impairments by minimizing stress and maximizing participation, especially in low-resource environments such as Sri Lanka. Brain-Computer Interfaces provide real-time information regarding students' emotional and cognitive states to customize lessons better, but more study is necessary for application in ASD [7]. Besides this, robotic interventions support the growth of emotional and social skills through engaging and structured interactions.

Psychomotor Domain:

In the area of psychomotor activities, which encompasses movement, coordination, and the development of motor development, these children might experience delays. This, in turn, precludes the children from being able to follow and carry out instructions in their daily activities. Technology has brought forth various kinds of tools that help with these skills. Motion-sensing cameras like the Microsoft Kinect and Leap Motion are usable in therapeutic games to improve motor functions through two-way interaction of body movements and real-time feedback. Gesture-based learning systems, integrated with computer vision and machine learning, maintain an adaptive difficulty level suited to each child's progress [8]. Wearable sensors and smart gloves infer fine motor movements and inform training sessions, which are adapted based on user performance data. Robotic-assisted therapy also uses robots such as NAO and Kaspar for physical action modelling and to teach/improve imitation skills through reinforcement learning. These AR and VR games further develop spatial awareness, motor coordination, and balancing skills by compelling physical interaction with digital objects[9]. Such advanced tools render motor-skill development programs engaging, adaptive, and effective for children with ASD

All in all, it is evident from the reviewed literature that a growing potential is being identified for the use of machine learning and intelligent systems to enhance the learning experience of children with ASD in the domains mentioned above. Each domain presents its own challenges to teach these children, and technology offers solutions to these challenges in some ways. These insights become the basis upon which a customized learning platform-based mode can be formed that adapts to the needs of each child and enhances their learning and emotional development in this way.

Current educational interventions for children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) face several significant limitations that hinder their effectiveness and scalability. Most existing approaches lack the integration of machine learning techniques, which could enable more sophisticated pattern recognition and adaptive learning systems. Additionally, these interventions typically offer generic, one-size-fits-all activities rather than providing customized activities tailored to individual learning needs and preferences.

A critical gap exists in the classification of learning domains into different difficulty levels, making it challenging to provide appropriate progressive learning pathways for children with varying abilities. Furthermore, existing systems are predominantly static and do not adapt based on user inputs, limiting their ability to respond to individual progress and changing needs.

Perhaps most importantly, the absence of real-time feedback mechanisms prevents immediate assessment and adjustment of learning activities, reducing the potential for timely intervention and support. This lack of immediate response capability significantly impacts the learning experience and outcomes for children with ASD, who often benefit greatly from instant reinforcement and guided correction.

These gaps collectively represent a significant opportunity for developing more effective, personalized, and responsive educational tools for children with autism spectrum disorders.

The lack of knowledge among peers and parents surrounding autism means misguidance, poor support, and barriers to socialization for the autistic child. Both parents and peers are often unable to see a child’s unique learning and emotional abilities or needs and may misinterpret the child’s response. If there is no similar understanding of the child, structured routines, communication aids, and behaviour management strategies may not be implemented, and the child will struggle to transition to social and educational settings. In addition, lack of awareness contributes to social rejection, bullying, and inconsistent support at home and at school. There needs to be a structured way for schools and communities to provide awareness, peer education, or parental support groups. When awareness is structured and encouraged, autistic children can create skills they need to navigate their classroom and social environments.

  • Understand How Autistic Children Learn
    •  LearnMate aims to deeply understand how autistic children take in, process, and act on information across four domains: cognitive, metacognitive, psychomotor, and affective. This helps us build a holistic educational model tailored to each child’s unique learning style.

  • Design Personalized Learning Plans
    • Using behavioral data and engagement tracking, LearnMate creates customized learning pathways that adapt to each child’s current abilities and future growth. Machine learning helps recommend activities that are intellectually engaging, emotionally safe, physically manageable, and mentally stimulating.

  • Support Differentiated Instruction
    •  Teachers and caregivers receive targeted tools and strategies across all learning domains, including visual aids, self-monitoring tools, hands-on tasks, and emotional supports. AI also recommends specific strategies based on each child’s needs.

  • Ensure Flexibility Across Environments
    • LearnMate's plans are modular and portable, so children can use the same supports at school, home, or therapy sessions. The approach ensures emotional safety, routine consistency, and adaptable learning experiences across all settings.

  • Empower and Train Educators
    •  Teachers get the training and technology they need to support autistic learners in every domain. LearnMate acts as a digital teaching partner—suggesting actions based on real-time behavioral data.

  • Respect the Rights and Dignity of Children
    •  All interventions are child-centered, ethical, and respectful. We avoid invasive tools and focus on behavioral patterns and caregiver input. The goal is to help autistic children thrive while preserving their individuality and autonomy.

When the project topic was chosen, a feasibility analysis was conducted. A feasibility study was used to evaluate the viability of the idea, including analysing the project’s technical feasibility, credibility, and restrictions. A feasibility analysis evaluates the project’s likelihood of success. As part of the feasibility studies, technical feasibility was evaluated to determine the availability of technical resources for the team. It helps the team determine the project’s potential benefits. A legal feasibility assessment was conducted to examine the project’s potential compliance with data protection, social, and medical legislation. It was determined through an operational feasibility analysis how well a project plan fits system development requirements. A scheduled feasibility analysis was conducted to evaluate how long it would take to execute the project.

Before beginning the actual coding, it is essential to understand how the “now” program will help the end-user and to have a clear understanding of what the ultimate product should look like. This phase evaluates the preceding phase’s requirements specifications and generates a system design. System design contributes to the design of the overall system architecture and description of hardware and system requirements. Therefore, functional and non-functional requirements were identified.

As the first step of data gathering, meetings were held with professionals such as psychiatrists and doctors specialized in ASD as well as educators such as teachers and caregivers in order to identify the problems they face when dealing with children with ASD and other requirements. Upon the resources gathered using the help of these professionals, parameters were identified for each domain to collect relevant data.

Data relating to these four learning domains were gathered from various hospitals and learning centres specialized in serving children with Autistic Spectrum Disorder. Further to that, data was collected through an online survey from parents of these children as well. The design of LearnMate represents a careful balance of technical sophistication and user-centered simplicity, tailored specifically for children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD) and their educators. The application's architecture, as illustrated in the above diagram, integrates advanced machine learning capabilities with an accessible, intuitive interface designed to accommodate the unique needs of children with autism. The application leverages a modern cloud-based infrastructure with multiple interconnected components. Authentication services ensure secure access for both educators and children, while the API Gateway serves as the central communication hub between the web client and mobile interfaces. MongoDB provides a flexible database solution capable of storing and retrieving the complex, multidimensional data necessary for personalized learning pathways. The application features two distinct yet complementary interfaces. The educator dashboard provides comprehensive progress visualizations and detailed analytics, designed for maximum information density while maintaining clarity and accessibility. This allows teachers and caregivers to track developmental progress, identify challenge areas, and evaluate the effectiveness of interventions.

Model Training

The collected data were pre-processed accordingly before using them in machine learning model training. At least 4 different models were used in each learning domain. After numerous rounds of hyper-parameter tuning, these models were trained with optimized parameters. Then the best models for each learning domain is selected based on the evaluation metrics such as Accuracy, error, F1-Score, Recall and Precision.

Recommendations Creation

By categorizing children according to different age ranges and domain levels (mild, moderate and severe) each recommendation is appropriately contextualized to align with children's capacity and learning needs. All activities are designed to be inclusive of all autistic children to avoid exclusion by cognitive or motor impairments. The activities are primarily based on strengthening key learning skills. Each recommendation is fully articulated, using simple terms and depictions, and incorporates step-by-step instructions to facilitate understanding and implementation. The effectiveness of the recommendations will be monitored as children progress and will be adjusted accordingly.

Progress Tracking

The percentage of progress will be calculated for each recommended activity and stored. The concluding point-in-time percentage for progress will be calculated as an average across all recommendation activities. Parents will then enter the performance metrics for your child on a weekly basis. As data is logged, the system will track and visualize the percentage increase/decrease each day for each activity in a line graph, which will illustrate the percentage progress report over time to the parent and allow each to observe trends over time and determine whether ongoing support or increased focus is warranted and make recommendations based on the child's continued progress.

Frontend implementation

A comprehensive chart showing the progress of the individuals is presented in detail and displayed to responsible personnel, similar to educators and/or teachers. The component of the dashboard is significant as it requires the display of all necessary data, which must all be viewable in a simplified manner. Following this, there is a change in the interface where the child is given direct, personalized recommendations specific to them as the individual. This is designed to allow the child to begin and complete these activities while remaining simple, obvious, and appropriate to a child. Considering that autistic children are sensitive to different stimuli; the application is designed with soft colours and a touch of warm tones. The complete set of UI elements, including buttons, forms, and activities, has been kept simple, as the primary users are care-givers and teachers.
